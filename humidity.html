    • Humidity

    Humidity is the amount of water vapour present in air. Water vapour, the gaseous state of water, is generally invisible to the human eye. Humidity indicates the likelihood for precipitation, dew, or fog to be present. The amount of water vapour needed to achieve saturation increases as the temperature increases.

    The results of my data show that near the equator there is a significantly higher liklihood of higher humidty. Cities on the equator all had humidity percentages above 80. The further away from the equator there appears to be greater variance in humidity and a higher liklihood of less humidity.
